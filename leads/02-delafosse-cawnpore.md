# Lead 02: Henry George Delafosse — Cawnpore Massacre Survivor

## Status: Researched (2026-03-21)

## What We Know (Pre-Research Baseline)

- **Major-General Henry George Delafosse, CB** (1835--1905)
- One of only four survivors of the **Cawnpore (Kanpur) Massacre, 27 June 1857** during the Indian Rebellion
- Son of **Henry Delafosse** (1794--1845), Bengal Artillery officer who died of sunstroke at Lakki-ki-Dera, Ferozepur, Punjab
- Grandson of **Robert Mark Delafosse** (1757--1819, clergyman at Richmond) and **Jane Theresia d'Abbadie** (1760--1822)
- Brother **Claude Fraser Delafosse** (1868--1950) baptised at Futtegurh, India
- The database notes say "CB" but doesn't specify which gazette

## Findings

### 1. Henry George Delafosse: Full Biography

**Birth:** 24 April 1835, Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh, India (WikiTree gives 1835; Geni.com gives 1836 -- the *Dictionary of Indian Biography* confirms 1835).

**Parents:** Major Henry Delafosse, CB (11 March 1794, Richmond, Surrey -- 3 October 1845, Lakki-ki-Dera, India) and Louisa Maria Shield (1808--1887, Chelsea). They married 17 April 1827 at Islington, Middlesex. Louisa was the eldest daughter of George Shield and his wife Sarah Fabian.

**Education:** Addiscombe Military Seminary (the East India Company's officer training college near Croydon, Surrey). This institution closed in 1861.

**Entered the Army:** 1854 (aged ~19), commissioned into the Bengal service.

**Regiment at Cawnpore:** Lieutenant in the **53rd Bengal Native Infantry** (53rd BNI). Note: Mowbray Thomson's published memoir is subtitled *"The Indian Mutiny 1857 of the 53rd Native Infantry, Bengal Army"*, confirming both men served in the same regiment.

**Death:** 10 February 1905, St Helier, Jersey. (Some sources give simply "February 1905".)

**Wife:** Helen Scott Fraser. (The Fraser connection explains the middle name of his brother Claude Fraser Delafosse.)

**Children:** At least one daughter, **Margaret Maud Delafosse**, documented in the Cobbold Family History Trust database. She was the mother of Patrick Henry Delafosse Dempsey.

### 2. The Cawnpore Massacre and Escape (27 June 1857)

The siege of Cawnpore lasted from 5--25 June 1857 under General Sir Hugh Wheeler. On 27 June, the surviving garrison (including women and children) were promised safe passage down the Ganges. At Satichaura Ghat, as the British boarded boats, Nana Sahib's forces opened fire. This became known as the Cawnpore Massacre.

**The four male survivors who escaped by river:**
1. **Lieutenant Henry George Delafosse**, 53rd BNI
2. **Lieutenant Mowbray Thomson**, 53rd BNI (later General Sir Mowbray Thomson, KCIE; died 1917)
3. **Private Dennis Murphy** (sometimes "Murphey"), 84th Regiment of Foot
4. **Gunner Sullivan**, 1st Madras Fusiliers

The four men swam downstream in the Ganges for approximately two and a half hours. They reached shore and were found by Rajput matchlock-men loyal to **Raja Digvijay Singh of Balrampur**, a British loyalist. Nana Sahib reportedly tried three times to compel the Raja to hand over the survivors, but the Raja refused, declaring he did not recognise Nana's authority.

After several weeks recuperating at the Raja's palace, the survivors eventually reached safety by joining the relief force advancing up the Ganges.

**Subsequent fates of the four:**
- **Murphy** died shortly after from cholera
- **Sullivan** also died shortly after from cholera
- **Delafosse** joined the defending garrison at the **Siege of Lucknow** (making him a survivor of TWO major sieges of 1857)
- **Thomson** participated in rebuilding and defending the entrenchment under General Windham, and later wrote his famous memoir

Thomson later said he owed his survival to **Holborn Baths**, where he had learned to swim as a boy.

### 3. Published Accounts and Memoirs

**Delafosse's own account:** Multiple sources (particularly the Mutiny Reflections blog) state that Delafosse **"left behind a somewhat disjointed account of Cawnpore and could never be convinced to write a book."** This implies a fragmentary written account exists (perhaps in manuscript or in excerpted form in other works) but Delafosse never published a full memoir. The location of this "disjointed account" is not identified in online sources -- it may survive in the Jersey Heritage archive collection (see below) or in the British Library's India Office Records.

**Mowbray Thomson's memoir:** *The Story of Cawnpore* (London: R. Bentley, 1859). Available on Internet Archive, Google Books, and HathiTrust. This is the primary first-hand published account of the massacre from a survivor's perspective. Delafosse features in Thomson's narrative.

**Modern works:**
- James W. Bancroft, *The Devil's Trap: The Victims of the Cawnpore Massacre During the Indian Mutiny* (Pen & Sword, 2018)
- The Mutiny Reflections blog series at mutinyreflections.com provides detailed modern analysis

### 4. Military Career After 1857

Per the *Dictionary of Indian Biography* (Wikisource, p.131):
- **1854:** Entered the Army (commissioned from Addiscombe)
- **1857:** Lieutenant, 53rd BNI, at Cawnpore; survived the massacre; served with Havelock's relief force at the First Relief of Lucknow; served in the Defence of Lucknow
- **By 1884:** Colonel H. G. Delafosse, CB, commanding No. 25 Volunteer Battalion at Berwick-on-Tweed (per army distribution lists)
- **1887:** Promoted to Major-General; retired from the army the same year
- **1905:** Died at St Helier, Jersey, 10 February

**Note on his father's CB:** The *Dictionary of Indian Biography* describes H.G. Delafosse as "son of Major Henry Delafosse, C.B." -- meaning the CB belonged to the FATHER, not necessarily to Henry George himself. However, online genealogical sources and the 1884 army list both append "CB" to Henry George's own name, suggesting both father and son held the honour. This needs clarification from the London Gazette.

**Note on "VC" attribution:** The Cobbold Family History Trust describes Henry George Delafosse as holding the "VC" (Victoria Cross). However, **he does not appear on any published list of Indian Mutiny Victoria Cross recipients**. This attribution appears to be erroneous in the Cobbold database. He is not listed in Wikipedia's "List of Indian Mutiny Victoria Cross recipients." The honour should be understood as CB (Companion of the Bath), not VC.

### 6. Henry Delafosse, CB (1794--1845) -- The Father

- **Born:** 11 March 1794, Richmond, Surrey, England
- **Died:** 3 October 1845, Lakki-ki-Dera, India (confirmed on Geni.com)
- **Service:** Bengal Artillery officer. Held the rank of Major and the CB (Companion of the Bath).
- **Marriage:** Louisa Maria Shield, 17 April 1827, Islington, Middlesex
- **Children (known):** Mary Isabella Delafosse (b. 25 Dec 1833, Kanpur; married Bond; d. 1916), Henry George Delafosse (b. 1835), Charles Edward Delafosse (b. 24 July 1838, Mathura; d. 24 May 1868), Claude Fraser Delafosse (b. 1868 -- see note below)

**Important note on Claude Fraser Delafosse (1868--1950):** He cannot be a son of Henry Delafosse senior (d. 1845) since he was born 23 years after his supposed father's death. Claude is more likely a son or nephew of Henry George Delafosse, or of another sibling. His birth year (1868) and the death of Charles Edward Delafosse (also 1868) is striking. FamilySearch confirms Claude as "Sir Claude Fraser Delafosse, Kt, CIE, MA (1868--1950)." He authored *History of India* (1917), available on Internet Archive.

### 7. Robert Mark Delafosse (1757--1819) -- The Grandfather

- **Born:** c. November 1757, Brentford, Middlesex
- **Died:** 27 July 1819
- **Role:** Clergyman in Richmond, Surrey. He served at **Petersham Church (St Peter's, Petersham)**, where his son Daniel Charles Delafosse later assisted him as curate after ordination in 1809. The family is associated with the Richmond/Petersham area.
- **Wife:** Jane Theresia (Theresa) d'Abbadie (the d'Abbadie connection suggesting Huguenot or French Protestant ancestry)
- **Library sale:** After his death, his "very Valuable Library" was auctioned by **Sotheby's on 20 October 1819** (a Google Play Books record of the catalogue exists)

**Note on St Mary Magdalene, Richmond:** The lead states Robert Mark Delafosse was clergyman at St Mary Magdalene, Richmond, but the sources found specifically name **Petersham Church (St Peter's, Petersham)** as his church. Petersham is adjacent to Richmond and in the same parish complex. St Mary Magdalene, Richmond, is the main parish church. He may have served at both, or the attribution may need checking. The Wikipedia article on St Mary Magdalene, Richmond, does not mention Delafosse in its web-accessible summary.

### 8. The Wider Delafosse Clerical Family

- **Rev. Daniel Charles Delafosse, MA** (3 June 1784 -- 21 March 1859): Eldest son of Robert Mark Delafosse. Eton and King's College, Cambridge (BA 1808). Ordained 1809. Assisted his father at Petersham. Schoolmaster in Richmond. Vicar of Wandsworth from 1838. Rector of Shere (St James's Church, Shere, Surrey) from 1844 until death. A ceramic memorial plaque at **All Saints Church, Wandsworth** reads "D. C. Delafosse, A.M. -- Vicar" (1841).

- **John Roberts Delafosse** (1786--1856): Another son of Robert Mark, per Ancestry records.

- **Rev. Edward Hollingworth Delafosse:** A naval officer (not a clergyman despite the "Rev." sometimes attributed). Served at the Battle of Copenhagen (1801) and the Battle of Algiers (1816). Promoted Commander. His exact relationship to the Richmond Delafosses is not confirmed in online sources but the surname and era suggest kinship.

### 9. Monuments and Memorials

- **All Souls Memorial Church (Kanpur Memorial Church), Kanpur:** Both Delafosse and Thomson have "separate monuments" inside the church. The church was built in 1875 in Lombardy Gothic style by architect Walter Granville. It contains 14 memorial tablets behind the altar with over 1,000 names commemorating the dead.

- **All Saints Church, Wandsworth:** Memorial plaque for Rev. D. C. Delafosse (uncle of Henry George).

- **St Peter's Church, Petersham:** Associated with Rev. Robert Mark Delafosse (grandfather).

- **Burial of Henry George Delafosse:** Likely in Jersey (he died at St Helier, 10 February 1905). No Find a Grave memorial was located online. The Jersey Heritage archive holds records catalogued under his name.

### 10. Jersey Heritage Archive

The Jersey Heritage catalogue (catalogue.jerseyheritage.org) has records indexed under the name **"de La Fosse, Henry George, Major General"** and also under **"Bengal Artillery"**. This suggests the archive holds papers, correspondence, or documents related to the Delafosse military family. These may include the "disjointed account" of Cawnpore mentioned by other sources.

**Recommended action:** Contact Jersey Heritage (jerseyheritage.org) to request details of the de La Fosse archive holdings.

### 11. Huguenot / French Origins

The surname "De la Fosse" (later anglicised to Delafosse) is of French origin. WikiTree records show early ancestors including Barthelemy de la Fosse (b. 1669, Rouen, Normandie, France) and Marie Josepha de la Fosse (b. 1674, Noyon, Picardie, France). The marriage of Robert Mark Delafosse to Jane Theresia d'Abbadie reinforces a French/Huguenot family network in 18th-century England. The d'Abbadie surname is a well-known Huguenot/French Protestant name.
