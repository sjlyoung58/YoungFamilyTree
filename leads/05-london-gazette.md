## Findings

### 1. DELAFOSSE — Military Gazette Entries

**Note:** The surname appears as both "Delafosse" and "De la Fosse" in gazette records. Both spellings should be searched.

#### H.G. Delafosse — Colonel, Half-Pay (3 December 1886)
- **Issue:** 25652, **Page:** 6126
- Lieutenant-Colonel and Colonel H. G. Delafosse, C.B., placed on half-pay after completing five years commanding No. 25 Regimental District (King's Own Borderers)
- **PDF:** https://www.thegazette.co.uk/London/issue/25652/page/6126/data.pdf

#### H.G. Delafosse — Probable Retirement/Promotion (~May 1890)
- **Issue:** 26052, **Page:** 2900
- Likely relates to retirement or promotion to Major-General on the retired list
- **PDF:** https://www.thegazette.co.uk/London/issue/26052/page/2900/data.pdf

#### Probable Earlier Delafosse Entries
- **Issue 23636, page 3480** (22 July 1870) — may contain a promotion during Bengal Staff Corps service
- **Issue 24668, page 175** (January 1879) — Bengal military appointments dated 31 Dec 1878

#### Henry Delafosse (1794–1845) — CB
- **NOT FOUND** via web search. The CB (gazetted c.1842–43) is in the pre-Victorian period where gazette entries are poorly indexed online. Direct search at thegazette.co.uk for "Delafosse" / "De la Fosse" with date range 1842–1845 recommended.

#### Additional Discovery: Edward Hollingworth Delafosse
- Royal Navy Commander, promoted 16 September 1816
- Fought at Copenhagen (1801), Algiers (1816), served as Inspecting Commander of the Coast Guard in Dorsetshire
- This is Edward Hollingworth Delafosse (1788–?) from the family tree — Robert Mark Delafosse's son

### 2. REGINALD COPLESTONE BOND — DSO Confirmed

Reginald Coplestone Bond DSO (1866–1936), King's Own Yorkshire Light Infantry. His mother was **Mary Isabella, née De La Fosse** — confirming the Delafosse family connection.

#### DSO — Boer War (27 September 1901) — CONFIRMED
- **Issue:** 27359, **Page:** 6318
- Created Companion of the Distinguished Service Order for services in the Boer War: advance on Kimberley; actions at Belmont, Enslin, Modder River, Magersfontein. Also mentioned in despatches.
- **PDF:** https://www.thegazette.co.uk/London/issue/27359/page/6318/data.pdf

#### Other Probable Bond Entries
- **Captain** (~October 1894) — Issue 26559, page 5690
- **Major** (~October 1902) — Issue 27490, page 6901 (brevet promotion after South Africa)
- **Commissioned** November 1888 — 2nd Lieutenant, KOYLI (specific issue not identified)
- **Lieutenant-Colonel** May 1914 — commanding 2nd Battalion KOYLI (specific issue not identified)

### 3. MULLORD — Business Notices

#### Multum in Parvo Partnership Dissolution (probable: 4 March 1890)

### 4. ERNEST COOKE — Military Medal — CONFIRMED

- **Issue:** 37284, **Supplement page:** 4781
- **Date:** 27 September 1945
- Lists "Sergeant Ernest COOKE, Corps of Royal Engineers" (service number 2121422)
- **PDF:** https://www.thegazette.co.uk/London/issue/37284/supplement/4781/data.pdf
- Full citation (from COPP Survey / WO 373/98/60): *"Sergeant Cooke has taken part in five operations of a protracted nature in less than three weeks, and in enemy held territory. During most of these operations he was in very close proximity to the enemy but continued his reconnaissances with coolness, courage and skill."*
