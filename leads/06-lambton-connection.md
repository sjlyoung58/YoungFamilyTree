# Lead 06: Lambton Connection — Earls of Durham?

## Status: CONFIRMED — Direct Connection to the Earls of Durham

## What We Know (from the Young Family Tree database)

- **Ralph Lambton** (1652–1717) — his daughter Anne Lambton (b. 1701) married Robert Chilton
- Their daughter Dorothy Chilton (1730–1806, buried Iver Church, Bucks) married Robert Young, bringing the Lambton name into the Young line
- The "Lambton" middle name persists through: George Chilton Lambton Young, Thomas Chilton Lambton Young, Victor Edward Lambton Young, Simon John Lambton Young
- Database note on Ralph Lambton: _"This tree also connects to the Hedworths with the marriage of Margaret Lambton (b. c1444) and Robert Hedworth (b. 1435). Thomas Lambton (b. c1408) is the father of Margaret Lambton and William Lambton (b. 1442) who is the 5xGreat-grandfather of (this) Ralph Lambton"_

## Research Questions & Answers

### 1. Is Ralph Lambton (1652–1717) connected to the Lambton family of Lambton Castle?

**YES — he is not merely "connected" but is a direct member of the main Lambton line.**

Ralph Lambton (1652–1717) of Barnes, County Durham, was the younger son of Henry Lambton of Lambton (1615–1693) and Mary Davison (daughter of Sir Alexander Davison of Blakiston). His elder brother was **William Lambton (1640–1724)**, who held Lambton Castle and served as MP for County Durham.

William Lambton died unmarried in 1724. Ralph Lambton's eldest son, **Henry Lambton (1697–1761)**, inherited the Lambton estate from his uncle William. Henry also died unmarried. The estate then passed to another of Ralph's sons, William, who also died unmarried in 1774. It then passed to a third son, **General John Lambton (1710–1794)**, who married Lady Susan Lyon, daughter of Thomas Lyon, 8th Earl of Strathmore.

General John Lambton's grandson was **John George Lambton, 1st Earl of Durham (1792–1840)** — "Radical Jack."

**Therefore, Ralph Lambton (1652–1717) of Barnes is the grandfather of General John Lambton and the great-great-grandfather of the 1st Earl of Durham. Anne Lambton (b. 1701), who married Robert Chilton and brought the Lambton name into the Young family, was a sister of General John Lambton's father (i.e. a daughter of Ralph Lambton of Barnes).**

### 2. Is there a common ancestor with the Earls of Durham?

**YES — Ralph Lambton (1652–1717) himself is the common ancestor.** The Earls of Durham descend from Ralph Lambton's son General John Lambton. The Young family descends from Ralph Lambton's daughter Anne Lambton (who married Robert Chilton).

The 1st Earl of Durham and Dorothy Chilton (who married Robert Young) were therefore **first cousins once removed** — Dorothy being the granddaughter of Anne Lambton, while the 1st Earl was the great-grandson of General John Lambton, Anne's brother.

### 3. Can Thomas Lambton (b. c.1408) be verified in published Lambton pedigrees?

**YES — comprehensively verified.** Thomas Lambton (c.1408–1473) appears in multiple authoritative sources:

- **Durham Visitations** (1575, 1615, 1666) recorded by successive Norroy Kings of Arms (William Flower, Richard St George, William Dugdale) include pedigrees for "Lambton of Lambton"
- **WikiTree** (Lambton-3): Thomas Lambton, born c.1408 in Lambton, Durham, died 26 September 1473
- **Geni.com**: Thomas de Lambton, Lord of Lambton (1408–1473)
- **Inquisition Post Mortem** (20 October 1473, Chester-le-Street): confirms William Lambton, aged 36, as Thomas's son and next heir
- Thomas was the son of **William de Lambton (c.1390–c.1432)** and Elizabeth, and held lands at Pencher, Bishop-Houghton, and the fishery of Shiphaugh
- Thomas was "heir to his brother" (implying an elder brother died without issue), had livery 3 April 1442, and was a Commissioner of Array for Chester Ward in 1455

### 4. Is the Hedworth connection documented?

**YES.** The Hedworth connection is documented at two separate points:

**Medieval connection (c.1444):** Margaret Lambton (b. c.1444), daughter of Thomas Lambton (c.1408–1473), married Robert Hedworth (b. c.1435) of Harraton, Durham. Their son was Sir John Hedworth (b. c.1460, d.1534) of Harraton. This is confirmed in Ancestry.com records and in the Hedworth of Harraton pedigree documented in Robert Surtees' *History and Antiquities of the County Palatine of Durham* (vol. 2, 1820).

**Later connection (1696):** Ralph Lambton (1652–1717) married Dorothy Hedworth, daughter of John Hedworth of Harraton, in 1696. Through this marriage the Hedworth estates at Harraton passed into Lambton hands, consolidating lands on both banks of the River Wear. This Hedworth family were descendants of the same Hedworth of Harraton line. So the Lambton and Hedworth families intermarried twice — once in the 1440s and again in 1696.

## The "5x Great-grandfather" Claim

The database note says Thomas Lambton (b. c.1408) was "father of William Lambton (b. 1442) who is the 5xGreat-grandfather of (this) Ralph Lambton."

Counting the generations:
1. William Lambton (c.1442) → 2. William Thomas Lambton (c.1467) → 3. John Lambton (c.1505) → 4. Robert Lambton (c.1533) → 5. Ralph Lambton (c.1554) → 6. Col. Sir William Lambton (1589) → 7. Henry Lambton (1615) → 8. Ralph Lambton (1652)

That gives William Lambton (1442) as the **6x great-grandfather** of Ralph Lambton (1652), not 5x. Either the database note miscounts by one generation, or there is a collapsed generation in some versions of the pedigree. The lineage itself is sound; the generation count is slightly off.

## Heraldry

The Lambton arms are: **Sable, a fesse between three lambs passant argent.** Crest: a ram's head cabossed argent, attired sable. These arms are recorded in the Durham Visitations for both "Lambton of Lambton" and the cadet branch "Lambton of Belsis."

The name Lambton itself derives from the place — "lamb farm settlement" — explaining the canting arms.

---

## The Lambton of Belsis Branch

The Durham Visitations record a separate cadet branch: **Lambton of Belsis**, descending from a younger son (Robert Lambton, then Percival Lambton who married Elizabeth Clenaulx of Croft). This is a distinct junior line from the main Lambton of Lambton family. Ralph Lambton of Barnes does NOT belong to this cadet branch — he is from the main line.

## Assessment of Plausibility

**The connection is not merely plausible — it is well-documented and confirmed by multiple independent sources.**

The Ralph Lambton (1652–1717) in the Young family tree is the same Ralph Lambton of Barnes, County Durham, who was:
- A younger son of Henry Lambton of Lambton (1615–1693) — the head of the main Lambton line
- Brother of William Lambton MP (1640–1724) of Lambton Castle
- Father of Henry Lambton MP (1697–1761) who inherited Lambton Castle
- Father of General John Lambton (1710–1794), whose grandson became the 1st Earl of Durham
- Father of Anne Lambton (b. c.1701) who married Robert Chilton, ancestor of the Young family

The descent from Thomas Lambton (c.1408) is confirmed by the Inquisition Post Mortem of 1473, the Durham Visitations of 1575/1615/1666, and numerous published genealogies.

**The Young family, through the Chilton marriage, are genuinely connected to the Earls of Durham.** Anne Lambton was aunt to William Henry Lambton and great-aunt to "Radical Jack" himself.
