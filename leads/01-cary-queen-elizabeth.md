# Lead 01: Cary — Maid of Honour to Queen Elizabeth

## Status: Researched (initial) — 2026-03-21

## What We Know

- The earliest known wife in the Young patrilineal line is a **Cary** (first name unknown) who married **Christopher Young**, "Mercht in London" (not Robert as originally assumed — see Gemini research below).
- The Visitation of England and Wales (**Notes, Vol. 3, p. 10**) and **Surtees' History of Durham (Vol. 3)** both record him as marrying a _"daughter of Cary, a mayd of honour to Q. Eliz."_ The Visitation "dots out" her first name.
- The repository holds Vol. 5 of the Visitation (pp. 140–142) — the PDF needs reading to confirm exact wording.
- Robert Young (d. 1603/4) was the patriarch of Edderacres, Durham. His son **Christopher Young** was the London Merchant who married the Cary bride. Christopher's grandson **Robert Young** was Sheriff of Newcastle in 1647.
- Robert Young (Sheriff, 1647) confirmed in Wikipedia's Sheriff list and Newcastle City Council records.

### 2. Which Cary Family? — The Hunsdon Branch is Most Likely

Multiple Cary women served as maids of honour to Elizabeth I. The **Hunsdon Careys** (Elizabeth's cousins through Mary Boleyn) are the strongest candidates because of their deep connections to the North of England.

#### Known Cary Maids of Honour

| Name | Dates | Marriage |
|------|-------|----------|
| Catherine Carey | c.1550–1603 | Married Charles Howard, 1st Earl of Nottingham |
| Philadelphia Carey | 1563–1629 | Married Thomas Scrope, 10th Baron Scrope of Bolton |
| Margaret Carey | 1564–1605 | Married Sir Edward Hoby, 1582 |
| Elizabeth Carey | 1576–1635 | Married Sir Thomas Berkeley, 1596 |
| Anne Carey | d. 1622 | Married Sir Francis Lovell, 1601 |

In 1604, the Earl of Worcester noted among the presence chamber maids: "Cary, Myddellmore, Woodhowse, Gargrave, Roper" — confirming a Cary still served at the transition to James I's court.

#### Why the Hunsdon Branch Fits Newcastle

The Hunsdon Careys had **sustained connections to the North of England**:

- **Henry Carey, 1st Baron Hunsdon** (1526–1596) was Warden of the East March and Governor of Berwick-upon-Tweed (1568–1596)
- **John Carey, 3rd Baron Hunsdon** served as Chamberlain of Berwick (1585), Deputy Warden and Marshal
- **Robert Carey, 1st Earl of Monmouth** served as Warden of the East, West, and Middle Marches (1593–1603) and married Elizabeth Trevanion _in Berwick-upon-Tweed_ — she was previously wife of Sir Henry Widdrington of Widdrington Castle, Northumberland

A Durham University thesis examines the Carey family as border officers (1568–1603): http://etheses.dur.ac.uk/3811/

A daughter or niece of this family marrying a Newcastle merchant like Robert Young is entirely plausible geographically and socially.

#### The Devon Cary Branch — Also Possible

The Devon Carys of Cockington are the ancestral trunk from which the Hunsdon branch sprang. One Devon Cary married into Northumberland: **Margaret Carey (1496–1560)** of Clovelly married **Sir John Delaval** (1493–1562), Sheriff of Northumberland — showing Cary-North marriages occurred from the Devon branch too.

Published sources on the Devon Carys:
- Fairfax Harrison, _The Devon Carys_ (1920): https://archive.org/details/devoncarys01harr
- Henry Grosvenor Cary, _The Cary Family in England_ (1894): https://archive.org/details/caryfamilyinengl00cary

### 3. Prior Gemini AI Research (from `docs/Young_Carey_Research.docx`)

Simon's earlier research session with Gemini AI (saved in the repo) produced significant additional context that our web research independently corroborated:

#### The Correct Pedigree Structure
- **Robert Young** (d. 1603/4) — patriarch of Edderacres, Durham
- **Christopher Young** (Gen II) — "Mercht in London" — married the Cary bride (c.1585–1595)
- **Robert Young** (Gen IV) — Sheriff of Newcastle (1647)
- **Dr. Christopher Young** — Sheriff, married Sarah Maddison (Hostmen coal elite)

#### The Edderacres Acquisition Mechanism
The Gemini research uncovered how the Cary marriage facilitated the family's rise from London merchants to Durham gentry:
- **Edderacres** (originally "Edward's akers") in Easington, Durham, was **Church land** belonging to the Dean and Chapter of Durham Cathedral
- The Youngs held it via **ecclesiastical lease** — collecting tithes on behalf of the Church
- The Cary connection provided the **political patronage** needed to secure this lease (the Careys were the supreme authority in the North)
- From Dugdale's Visitation: the Sequestration Books (D. and C. Library) show _"The tithes of Edward's akers, letten to Wm. Yonge, 1664"_ — the estate was likely sequestrated during the Civil War and **restored after the return of Charles II**

#### The Henry VIII / Tudor Link
The Gemini research laid out the reasoning for a potential Tudor royal descent:
- **Henry Carey, 1st Baron Hunsdon** (1526–1596) was the son of **Mary Boleyn**, sister of Anne Boleyn
- Mary Boleyn was Henry VIII's mistress during the time of Henry Carey's conception
- Contemporary accounts, the King's lavish grants to the family, and Henry Carey's physical resemblance to the King have led historians (Alison Weir, Eric Ives) to conclude he was likely the **biological son of Henry VIII**
- If true, the Cary bride was a granddaughter of the King, and the Young family carries Tudor biological bloodline

#### Parallel Cary-Young Intermarriage
A **Christopher Cary** (merchant, Sheriff of Bristol, d. 1626) married **Lettice Young**, daughter of John Young — proving the Cary and Young families were actively intermarrying in the merchant class during the same period. This is a separate, well-documented couple (confirmed by Bristol records), not a confusion with our Christopher Young.

#### The Maddison / Newcastle Coal Connection
Dr. Christopher Young married **Sarah Maddison**, whose family were the elite "Hostmen" — the guild controlling Newcastle's coal exports. Her grandfather **Sir Lionel Maddison** has a famous monument in Newcastle Cathedral. This made the Youngs part of the oligarchy controlling England's most important economic engine.

#### Candidate Narrowing
The Gemini research narrowed the likely Cary candidates:
- **Primary daughters of 1st Baron** (Catherine, Philadelphia, Margaret, Elizabeth) all married Earls/Barons — unlikely to be recorded with a "dotted line" in a merchant's pedigree
- **Daughters of Sir Edmund Carey** (youngest son of 1st Baron) are the strongest candidates — Maids of Honour but without massive dowries, making a wealthy London merchant an attractive match
- **Daughters of Sir John Carey, 3rd Baron** (Governor of Berwick) — also strong candidates given the direct Northern connection

#### Key Search Leads from Gemini Research
- **Will of Sir Edmund Carey** (proved 1637) — may mention a "daughter Young"
- **Will of Sir John Carey, 3rd Baron Hunsdon** (proved 1617) — same
