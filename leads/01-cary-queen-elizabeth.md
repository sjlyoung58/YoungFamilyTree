# Lead 01: Cary — Maid of Honour to Queen Elizabeth

## Status: Researched (initial) — 2026-03-21

## What We Know

- The earliest known wife in the Young patrilineal line is a **Cary** (first name unknown) who married **Robert Young** (d. 1603) of Newcastle-upon-Tyne.
- A note from the "Visitation of England and Wales" records her as _"maid of honour to Queen Elizabeth."_
- The repository holds Vol. 5 of the Visitation (pp. 140–142) but the Cary reference may be from Vol. 4. This volume discrepancy needs resolving by reading the actual PDF.
- Robert Young's will is dated 6 January 1603.
- His son Robert was Sheriff of Newcastle in 1647 (confirmed in Wikipedia's Sheriff list and Newcastle City Council records).

---

## Findings

### 1. The Visitation Source

The "Visitation of England and Wales" is a 34-volume series edited by Joseph Jackson Howard (Maltravers Herald Extraordinary) and Frederick Arthur Crisp, published 1893–1921. Both relevant volumes are **freely available on Archive.org**:

- **Volume 4:** https://archive.org/details/visitationengla04unkngoog
- **Volume 5:** https://archive.org/details/visitationofengl05howa

The PDF at `resources/visitationofeng_Vol5_p140-142.pdf` needs to be read directly — it likely contains the exact pedigree with the Cary name and "maid of honour" notation. Install `poppler-utils` (`brew install poppler`) to render it, or browse Volume 5 pages 140–142 on Archive.org.

**Additional indexes:**
- WikiTree index of all pedigrees by volume: https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/Space:Visitations_Of_England_and_Wales
- WordPress indexing project: https://visitationsofenglandandwales.wordpress.com/

### 2. Which Cary Family? — The Hunsdon Branch is Most Likely

Multiple Cary women served as maids of honour to Elizabeth I. The **Hunsdon Careys** (Elizabeth's cousins through Mary Boleyn) are the strongest candidates because of their deep connections to the North of England.

#### Known Cary Maids of Honour

| Name | Dates | Marriage |
|------|-------|----------|
| Catherine Carey | c.1550–1603 | Married Charles Howard, 1st Earl of Nottingham |
| Philadelphia Carey | 1563–1629 | Married Thomas Scrope, 10th Baron Scrope of Bolton |
| Margaret Carey | 1564–1605 | Married Sir Edward Hoby, 1582 |
| Elizabeth Carey | 1576–1635 | Married Sir Thomas Berkeley, 1596 |
| Anne Carey | d. 1622 | Married Sir Francis Lovell, 1601 |

In 1604, the Earl of Worcester noted among the presence chamber maids: "Cary, Myddellmore, Woodhowse, Gargrave, Roper" — confirming a Cary still served at the transition to James I's court.

#### Why the Hunsdon Branch Fits Newcastle

The Hunsdon Careys had **sustained connections to the North of England**:

- **Henry Carey, 1st Baron Hunsdon** (1526–1596) was Warden of the East March and Governor of Berwick-upon-Tweed (1568–1596)
- **John Carey, 3rd Baron Hunsdon** served as Chamberlain of Berwick (1585), Deputy Warden and Marshal
- **Robert Carey, 1st Earl of Monmouth** served as Warden of the East, West, and Middle Marches (1593–1603) and married Elizabeth Trevanion _in Berwick-upon-Tweed_ — she was previously wife of Sir Henry Widdrington of Widdrington Castle, Northumberland

A Durham University thesis examines the Carey family as border officers (1568–1603): http://etheses.dur.ac.uk/3811/

A daughter or niece of this family marrying a Newcastle merchant like Robert Young is entirely plausible geographically and socially.

#### The Devon Cary Branch — Also Possible

The Devon Carys of Cockington are the ancestral trunk from which the Hunsdon branch sprang. One Devon Cary married into Northumberland: **Margaret Carey (1496–1560)** of Clovelly married **Sir John Delaval** (1493–1562), Sheriff of Northumberland — showing Cary-North marriages occurred from the Devon branch too.

Published sources on the Devon Carys:
- Fairfax Harrison, _The Devon Carys_ (1920): https://archive.org/details/devoncarys01harr
- Henry Grosvenor Cary, _The Cary Family in England_ (1894): https://archive.org/details/caryfamilyinengl00cary

## What Was NOT Found

1. **The first name of the Cary woman** — the Visitation PDF likely names her but needs manual review
2. **Robert Young's will** (6 Jan 1603) — not found in online probate indices. Durham probate records at https://reed.dur.ac.uk/ should be checked
3. **No online family tree** (WikiTree, Geni, Geneanet) connects a Cary woman to Robert Young of Newcastle
4. **Which specific Cary** she was — without her first name, we can't definitively link to a branch
