# Donald Young's notes on the family home

*Extracted from Confluence FamilyTree wiki (exported 2 November 2012)*

---

## My transcription of Grandpa's notes

The Young family lived at 9 Milton Road Gravesend from 1895 to 1921 and seven children were born therein.

The house comprised -

The front basement 6 feet below the pavement with area in front about 6 feet wide with cement steps down to a front door. Behind this room was a large coal and wood cellar, then stairs leading up to the ground floor which contained the office in front where Victor E L Young ran his business of rent and debt collection. He was ready to do anything for a £1 or two and became secretary of the [Star Bowkett](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Starr-Bowkett_Society) Building Society, the Master Builders Association, and to the Tradesmans Association. He was also elected by vote to audit the Council's books with a second elected man G E Penman. I have an idea that the fee was about £20 each. He never sold a house to my knowledge from when I can remember to when I entered the buisiness in 1921. He also taught Pitman's Shorthand System and several young ladies came in the evenings.

The family were born as follows -

1897 Gertrude  
1899 Cyril  
1901 Percy  
1903 Donald  
1905 Mabel  
1911 Bert  
1913 Bernard

Behind Father's office was another office let to a local Coal Merchant and managed by my Father's twin brother Julian.

Then came our kitchen and from this a scullery with brick floor, stone sink, copper. There was no ceiling, only the roof rafters and undersides of the slates. The WC was at the bottom of the garden about 60/70 feet from the back door - there was no water laid on, so every user had to take a bucket of water down to this bog as we called it.

On the first floor the front room was our drawing room which we only used at Xmas or special occasions. The back room was Gert's bedroom. On the second floor was a nice front room where Mother and Father slept with a third bed where I slept until Cyril went off to live at Strood. The back bedroom was occupied by Cyril and Percy. Lighting was by gas.

I remember when I was about 10 that Father had a clerk named Ellis but he was only employed for a few months. Most of the time Mother had to look after the office when Father was out and took the rent or other monies paid.

Father paid Mother £1-19-6 for housekeeping and this remained consistent for years and I remember seeing the entry in the office cash book when I started in the business in 1921.

Gertie left school about 1912 when she was about 14.5, I believe Father taught her shorthand and she assisted in the office for a few months and then went to work for W J King & Son Accountants in Wrotham Road.

Cyril left school about 1915 and I think he had reached the 6th form. He was accepted as junior clerk at one of the big banks at Rochester. Because of the rather crowded bedroom situation he lived at Northcote Road Strood with a cousin Mrs. Richards and shared a bedroom with Joe Richards who was about 6 years age. Towards the end of 1917 Cyril decided to give a false age and enlist in the Army and subsequently became a gunman in the anti-aircraft section and served in France.

In the meantime when Percy was about 14.5 he decided he would like to go to sea in the merchant navy (about 1917) and later became a Trinity House Pilot and went off in his thick navy blue uniform, promising to buy the younger members of the family cream horns when he came home on leave. It was a great mistake because when he got to Newcastle and had to load coal onto the ship he didn't like it at all and wrote a letter to Father to try and get his indenture cancelled. Father went to London and in view of the letter agreed for him to come home. He came home and I think he tried his luck in Father's office but it was not to his liking, so after a few weeks he went to Henleys Tyre & Cable works as a labourer. Later on he joined the Essex Police Force.

About 1919 Cyril was demobbed and came home. I believe he tried to go back to the Bank, but after having a medical examination, they rejected his application. Here again I believe it was something to do with....
