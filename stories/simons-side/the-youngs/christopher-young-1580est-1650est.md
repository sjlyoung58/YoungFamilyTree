Looking at the first Christopher Young above, we see a marriage to ".   .   .   . Cary, maid of honour to Queen Elizabeth". This clearly refers to Catherine Knollys (nee Carey), allegedly the illegitimate child of Mary Boleyn and Henry VIII.

We have a slight problem with a lack of dates in the pedigree above. Looking at the following table in which I have estimated the probable birth/death ages of Christopher Young and descendants of Catherine Carey (assuming a lifespan of 70 and children born to parents of age 20), it seems highly likely that to be of a similar age to Christopher, we would be talking about a Great Grandchild of Catherine Carey, definitely not Catherine Carey herself. I presume the ".   .   .   ." is notation meaning "descendant of", and if each "." represents a generation, then that would also indicate a Great Grandchild. As this is quite a famous family, we are likely to be looking for peripheral family members who haven't previously been traced down the generations.

Catherine Carey was maid of honour to Queens Anne of Cleves and Catherine Howard. Catherine was appointed Chief Lady of the Bedchamber to Queen Elizabeth I. 

Under [Elizabeth I](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elizabeth_I_of_England "Elizabeth I of England"), [maids of honour](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maid_of_honour) were at court as a kind of finishing school, with the hope of making a good marriage. Elizabeth Knollys was a maid of honour to Queen Elizabeth 1. Elizabeth Knollys was both Catherine's daughter and maid of honour to Queen Elizabeth, so it seems she is the Grandmother of Christopher Young's spouse. The second tree excerpt above in particular makes it more clear with a reference to 'daughter of Carye, a mayd of honour to Q.Eliz'.

Elizabeth Knollys married [Sir Thomas Leighton](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Leighton_(Governor)) and they had three children, one son and two daughters - [Elizabeth Leighton](http://thepeerage.com/p1231.htm#i12307) and [Anne Leighton](http://thepeerage.com/p3070.htm#i30700)

Elizabeth Leighton only had sons, so we can rule her out, Anne Leighton however had 12 children by [Sir John St.John](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sir_John_St_John,_1st_Baronet) -

1. Oliver St John (1612/1613 – November 1641 or 1642) married Catherine Vere, daughter of [Horace Vere, 1st Baron Vere of Tilbury](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horace_Vere,_1st_Baron_Vere_of_Tilbury "Horace Vere, 1st Baron Vere of Tilbury") and Mary Tracy, and had [Sir John St John, 2nd Baronet](http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Sir_John_St_John,_2nd_Baronet&action=edit&redlink=1 "Sir John St John, 2nd Baronet (page does not exist)") in 1648 (died 1657)
2. [Anne St John, Countess of Rochester](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anne_Wilmot,_Countess_of_Rochester "Anne Wilmot, Countess of Rochester") (5 November 1614 – 18 March 1696), married first [Sir Francis Henry Lee, 2nd Baronet](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lee_Baronets "Lee Baronets"), of Ditchley, and second [Henry Wilmot, 1st Earl of Rochester](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_Wilmot,_1st_Earl_of_Rochester "Henry Wilmot, 1st Earl of Rochester")
3. John St John (b. 24 March 1615) married Dorothy Ayloffe.
4. William St John (b. 1616)
5. Edward St John (26 February 1617 – died at the [Second Battle of Newbury](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Battle_of_Newbury "Second Battle of Newbury"), 1645)
6. **Barbara St John (b. 1618)**
7. Nicholas St John (29 March 1620 – 18 April 1639)
8. **Lucy St John (b. 1621)**
9. [Sir Walter St John, 3rd Baronet](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sir_Walter_St_John,_3rd_Baronet "Sir Walter St John, 3rd Baronet") of Lydiard Tregoze and of Battersea (1622 – 3 July 1708) married in 1651 his second cousin Johanna St John, daughter of Oliver St John of Longthorpe
10. Francis St John (1623 – 1624)
11. Elizabeth St John (1624 – 1629)
12. Thomas St John (1625 – (1630)
13. Henry St John, of Tandragee (July 1628 – September 1679) married his second cousin Catherine St John, daughter of Oliver St John, of Longthorpe

The above includes 2 daughters with no marriage recorded, Barabara and Lucy (Elizabeth having died aged 5). This is exactly what we set out to find, 'unattached' Great Granddaughters of Catherine Carey via her daughter Elizabeth Knollys.

[Painting of Anne Leighton](http://www.bbc.co.uk/arts/yourpaintings/paintings/anne-leighton-c-15911628-first-wife-of-sir-john-st-john-1s65271) giving her estimated d.o.b.

So it seems very likely the lineage of Christopher Young's wife is - [Barbara or Lucy St. John)](http://thepeerage.com/p2632.htm#i26311)  -> [Anne Leighton](http://thepeerage.com/p3070.htm#i30700) -> [Elizabeth Knollys](http://thepeerage.com/p1377.htm#i13762) -> [Catherine Carey](http://thepeerage.com/p10305.htm#i103043)

There is a Barbara Young in the first pedigree above, but in a preceding generation, so unless there was some confusion, this does not help as she would be mother-in-law to Barbara or Lucy St. John.
