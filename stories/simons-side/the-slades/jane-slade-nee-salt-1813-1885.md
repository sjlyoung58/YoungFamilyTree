## My Great (x3) Grandmother ([tree](http://sjly.co.uk/webtrees/hourglass.php?rootid=I737&ged=SYJY))

Jane Slade was at the heart of the family shipbuilding business in Polruan. She took control of the family business on her husband's death. She was the only woman shipbuilder in Cornwall and her legacy lived on through successive generations of shipbuilders, repairers and mariners, and in the ship named after her.

Jane’s story inspired Daphne du Maurier’s first novel *[The Loving Spirit](http://www.dumaurier.org/reviews-spirit.html)*.

As well as shipbuilding, Jane and Christopher were innkeepers of the Russell Inn

##### ![Scan](FigureheadJaneSlade.png)  Figurehead from the Schooner, *The Jane Slade*

The schooner named after Jane Slade was finally beached and rotted away in Pont Creek. The remnants of her hull can still be seen from the cliff opposite. Her figurehead was rescued by Daphne Du Maurier and was restored and fixed to the south west corner of her lovely home, Ferryside, at Bodinnick. Here it overlooks the river where the ship traded for most of her life.

Subject of a biographical book *Jane Slade of Polruan*by [Helen Doe](http://www.helendoe.co.uk/books.htm)
